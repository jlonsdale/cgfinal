a) What is the difference between Phong shading and Phong lighting (the Phong reflection model)?
Phong lighting includes the specular highlight.

b) What is the difference between flat shading, Gouraud shading, and Phong shading? List pros and cons of each. Is Gouraud or Phong shading the best method for simulating highlights? Explain.
Flat Shading is simple and computationally inexpensive. Well-suited for low-powered or real-time applications. Works reasonably well for flat or low-poly objects. However it doesn't capture smooth lighting transitions within a polygon, and is bad for rounded objects.
Gouraud Shading gives us smoother shading than flat shading yet is less computationally intensive as Phong shading. Works well for matte objects. However, it doesn't compute the specular highlight.
Phong Shading provides the most accurate example of lighting and glossy highlights.However is computationally most expensive and more complex to impliment than the previous two shading models.

c) What is the difference between a directional light and a point light?
A directional light emits light in a constant direction, like sunlight, with no distance attenuation and a point light radiates light uniformly in all directions from a specific point in space and exhibits distance attenuation.

d) Does the eye position influence the shading of an object in any way?
Yes, when considering the specular highlight.

e) What is the effect of setting the specular term to (0, 0, 0)?
